What is SocialKYC

SocialKYC allows you to create, manage and use social credentials. Social credentials are credentials for your social accounts (such as LinkedIn, Twitter, TikTok, Discord, GitHub, etc.), email addresses or phone numbers as they are being release over time.

A credential of your social identity is issued when you proved control of the respective social account, email address or phone number. You can use these credentials to access third party services for example for logging into services that accept such credential.

Therefore, you can

  • verify your digital identity by proving control of your social account, email address or phone number
  • receive a social credential for your control of this social account, address or number
  • store the social credential locally on your device in your wallet
  • save a proof for the validity of your credential on the KILT blockchain and
  • use these credentials and the proof for their validity for pages that allow their usage e.g. to log into their services or to leave your contact data.

SocialKYC forgets about the user and the credential as soon as the credential is issued. Personal data is not stored, siloed, or shared.

The data generated in and for your digital identities is stored locally on your device.

SocialKYC stores a hash of each credential on the KILT blockchain to enable third parties to check the validity of a credential whenever you decide to share or show it.

How does it work

First, you are asked to connect your wallet with SocialKYC which needs to be downloaded before starting. At launch, the Website supports only the Sporran Wallet, other wallets may follow over time. For those other wallets, the process might differ slightly.

The wallet allows you to create digital identities for which SocialKYC attests verifiable credentials; this attestation is done automatically by the Software. You can choose between a set of credentials to be attested. In a first phase, we gradually activate to support attestations for email addresses, Twitter accounts and DotSama names (pinning a name onto your addresses/identities in the Kusama and Polkadot ecosystem); credentials for further digital identities are planned to be released over time.

To start the attestation process you first need to agree to the terms of a specific attestation (e.g. costs and expiration date) by signing a request for attestation with your identity’s password.

In the beginning all credentials will be valid for a year and free of charge. This might change later, and costs and expiration date will then be shown and signed before each attestation process begins.

Next you receive a credential that is not yet verified. Depending on the type of credential you need to perform a challenge to prove ownership of the credential. These challenges will be as follows:

  • For email, you have to enter your email address you want to have attested. Then, you will receive a verification link that you need to click to show that you control the account.
  • For your Twitter account, you need to enter your Twitter handle. Subsequently, SocialKYC provides a message you need to post publicly on your Twitter account. SocialKYC will check for the posting and verify the credential after that.
  • For the DotSama name, you can pick a name.
    • No challenge is required. However more trust is generated when the user generates additional credentials with that same wallet identity since these will also be shown on KILT Stakeboard and potentially other platforms.
    • Also, even if we do not hinder you from using any name of your choosing, please be aware that you are limited in your name usage by other individuals or entities who are in rightful possession of names, trademarks etc. already and have them protected according to their local or other jurisdictions.

Once a credential is verified an attestation hash is anchored to the KILT blockchain.
